#1 for Golf

WinStar Golf Club

Two championship courses at the world's largest casino — 36 holes of serious golf.

WinStar World Casino is the clear #1 pick for golfers, and it's not close. Two championship 18-hole courses — Scissortail and Redbud — provide 36 holes of quality golf just steps from the world's largest gaming floor.

Scissortail Course

The Scissortail is WinStar Golf Club's signature course and the more challenging of the two. Named after Oklahoma's state bird, it features rolling terrain, strategic bunkering, and water hazards that test every part of your game. The conditioning is excellent — fairways are well-maintained and the greens are smooth and true. This is a course that serious golfers will want to play twice.

Redbud Course

The Redbud course is slightly more forgiving and accessible to mid-handicap players while still providing a quality championship experience. Named after Oklahoma's state tree, the layout features wider fairways than Scissortail with generous landing areas off the tee, but the approach shots and green complexes still demand precision.

Facilities

The golf club includes a full pro shop, practice range with grass and mat hitting stations, putting green, and a clubhouse grill. Cart GPS is standard. Lessons are available from PGA professionals on staff.

Stay-and-Play Packages

WinStar's stay-and-play packages bundle one or two nights at the 1,700+ room resort with 18 or 36 holes of golf, sometimes including dining credits and free play. These packages represent the best value for a golf-casino weekend and can be booked directly through the WinStar website. WinStar Rewards members get priority booking and potential rate discounts.

Pro tip: Book tee times 1–2 weeks in advance for weekends, especially during spring and fall. Weekday mornings offer the best availability and often lower rates. Sign up for WinStar Rewards before booking to access member pricing.

#2 for Golf

Cherokee Hills Golf Club

18 holes at Hard Rock Tulsa — the Tulsa-area option for golf and gaming.

Hard Rock Casino Tulsa offers the Cherokee Hills Golf Club, an 18-hole course operated by the Cherokee Nation. While it doesn't match WinStar's two-course operation, it's a solid option — especially for Tulsa-area golfers who want to combine a round with some casino action without driving 3+ hours to the border.

The Course

Cherokee Hills is a well-maintained 18-hole layout with enough challenge for skilled players and enough forgiveness for weekend golfers. The course features a mix of links-style holes and tree-lined fairways, with water coming into play on several holes. Greens are in good shape and the overall pace of play is reasonable.

Facilities

The club includes a pro shop, practice area, and a comfortable clubhouse. It's not as extensive as WinStar's golf operation, but everything you need for a quality day of golf is here.

The Perfect Tulsa Golf-Casino Day

Morning round at Cherokee Hills, lunch at one of Hard Rock's restaurants, afternoon on the gaming floor, evening concert at Hard Rock Live. It's a full day of entertainment that's hard to beat for Tulsa-area residents. And with 454 hotel rooms, you can turn it into an overnight easily.

Stay-and-Play

Hard Rock Tulsa offers golf packages that bundle a hotel room with a round at Cherokee Hills. These are particularly good value midweek when both room rates and green fees are lower. Contact the Hard Rock directly or check their website for current package availability.

Planning Your Trip

Golf-Casino Trip Tips

Best Time of Year

Oklahoma golf is best in spring (March–May) and fall (September–November) when temperatures are comfortable and courses are in peak condition. Summer can be brutally hot (100°F+), and winter rounds are hit-or-miss. The casino, of course, is perfect year-round.

What to Pack

Bring your golf clubs (or rent at the pro shop), golf shoes, and weather-appropriate layers. For the casino, dress casual — jeans and a collared shirt work everywhere. Oklahoma weather can change quickly, so check the forecast before your round.

Budget Planning

A golf-casino weekend at WinStar typically runs $300–$600 per person including one night hotel ($100–$200 your share), one round of golf ($60–$120), meals ($60–$100), and gambling budget ($100–$200). Stay-and-play packages can reduce the total by 15–25%.

For Groups

Golf-casino trips are outstanding for groups (bachelor parties, buddies' weekends, corporate outings). Both WinStar and Hard Rock can accommodate group tee times and block room bookings. Contact their group sales departments for custom packages.
