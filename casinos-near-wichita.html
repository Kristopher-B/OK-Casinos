Kansas Visitors

Tips for Wichita Casino Trips

What Kansas residents need to know about Oklahoma casino visits.

Why Wichita Goes to Oklahoma

Kansas has some smaller casinos (Kansas Star, Boot Hill), but Oklahoma's tribal casinos offer a completely different level of gaming. Riverwind and Hard Rock Tulsa both have far more games, better table game options, and bigger entertainment venues.

Best Day Trip: Riverwind

At 2.5 hours, Riverwind Casino in Norman is the easiest day trip from Wichita. Take I-35 South straight down, enjoy an afternoon and evening of gaming, and drive home. Riverwind is known for strong table game options and a lively atmosphere.

Best Overnight: Hard Rock Tulsa

For a full resort experience, Hard Rock Tulsa has the hotel, poker room, golf course, and Hard Rock Live concert venue. Combine it with a night out in Tulsa's vibrant downtown for a well-rounded weekend trip.

Two Casinos, One Weekend

Riverwind (Norman/OKC area) and Hard Rock (Tulsa area) are about 100 miles apart. You could visit Riverwind on Saturday, drive to Tulsa for Hard Rock on Sunday, then head back to Wichita. Both cities offer plenty to do beyond the casinos.

💡 Pro Tip: The Kansas Turnpike has tolls. Get a K-TAG for faster passage, or carry cash for toll booths. Once you cross into Oklahoma, the Turnpike system is separate — have a Pikepass or cash/card for Oklahoma tolls.

Are Oklahoma casinos better than Kansas casinos?
Oklahoma has far more casinos (130+ tribal gaming facilities) with larger gaming floors, more table game options, and bigger entertainment venues. Kansas has a handful of casinos that are smaller by comparison.

Most Oklahoma tribal casinos require age 18+ for electronic games. Some areas or properties may require 21+ for certain sections or alcohol service. Always check with the specific casino.
