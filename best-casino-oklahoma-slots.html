Slot Tips

Tips for Slot Players
in Oklahoma

Always Sign Up for Rewards First

This is the single most important thing to do before you play your first spin. Every dollar you put through a machine earns points toward free play, hotel stays, dining, and more. Not using a rewards card is literally throwing money away. At Choctaw, it's Choctaw Rewards (Ruby through Onyx tiers). At WinStar, it's WinStar Rewards (Club through Diamond).

Understand Class II vs Class III

Most Oklahoma slot machines are technically Class II (bingo-based). They look and play identically to Vegas-style Class III slots, but the outcome is determined by an electronic bingo draw. The practical experience is the same — random outcomes with published return-to-player percentages. Don't let anyone tell you "Oklahoma slots are rigged" — they're regulated by the Oklahoma Horse Racing Commission and tribal gaming commissions.

Set a Budget and Stick to It

Decide how much you're willing to lose before you sit down, and stop when you hit that number. Slots are entertainment with a house edge. The average return on Oklahoma machines is roughly 90–93%, meaning for every $100 you play through, you'll lose $7–$10 on average over time.

Try Different Denominations

Higher denomination machines generally have higher return-to-player percentages. A $1 machine typically returns more per dollar played than a penny machine. If your bankroll allows, consider stepping up from pennies to quarters or dollars for better theoretical returns.
