Where to Eat

Both have solid dining, but Hard Rock has more options as the bigger resort.

Hard Rock Tulsa offers multiple restaurants including McGill's on 19th (upscale dining), Toby Keith's I Love This Bar & Grill, a food court, and several quick-service spots. The brand-name recognition of the Hard Rock chain means quality bar and grill experiences with that rock-and-roll atmosphere.

Riverwind has a well-regarded buffet, a steakhouse, and several casual dining options. The food quality is good for the price point, and the Chickasaw Nation operates clean, well-run kitchens. For a quick evening trip, the dining is more than adequate.

Neither casino is going to win any Michelin stars, but both deliver solid food at reasonable casino prices. Hard Rock wins on variety and atmosphere; Riverwind wins on value and no-nonsense quality.

Location & Access

Which Is Easier to Reach?

Depends on where you live. Each serves its metro perfectly.

Hard Rock Tulsa is in Catoosa, about 15 minutes east of downtown Tulsa off I-44/Will Rogers Turnpike. Easy access from anywhere in the Tulsa metro, Green Country, and northeastern Oklahoma. It's also the closest major casino for visitors from northwest Arkansas (about 2 hours from Fayetteville) and southwestern Missouri.

Riverwind Casino sits just south of Norman on I-35, about 20 minutes from downtown OKC. For the 1.4 million people in the OKC metro, Riverwind is the most convenient quality casino option — an easy weeknight trip with no hotel needed. It's also a natural stop for anyone driving I-35 between Dallas and OKC.

The two casinos are about 105 miles apart (roughly 1.5–2 hours via I-44/Turner Turnpike). They serve completely different markets and there's almost no overlap in their customer base.
