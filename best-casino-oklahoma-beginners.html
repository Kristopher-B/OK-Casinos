What to Expect

Your First Casino Visit:
Step by Step

No jargon, no judgment. Here's exactly what happens from the parking lot to the gaming floor.

1. Arriving & Parking

Oklahoma's major casinos have massive free parking lots and garages. Valet is usually available for a tip. Walk in through the main entrance — there's no doorman checking you, no velvet rope. It's open and welcoming.

2. The ID Check

You'll need a valid government-issued photo ID (driver's license, passport, or military ID). Most Oklahoma casinos require 18+ to enter the gaming floor, but some areas or properties require 21+. Security may check your ID at the door or on the floor. Always bring ID.

3. Sign Up for the Rewards Club

Before you play a single dollar, find the Player's Club desk and sign up. It's free, takes 5 minutes, and earns you points on everything you spend. At Choctaw, you'll get a Choctaw Rewards card. At WinStar, a WinStar Rewards card. Insert it into every machine you play and present it at every table. This is free money — don't skip it.

4. Getting Cash

Most players use cash. ATMs are available on the floor but charge $3–$8 fees. Better: bring cash from your own bank before you arrive. You can also use the cage (the bank-like counter on the casino floor) for larger transactions. Choctaw Durant uniquely accepts cryptocurrency at the cage.

5. Playing Slots

Slot machines are the easiest games to play. Insert your rewards card, then insert cash (bills $1–$100). Press the "Bet" button and "Spin." The machine handles everything. When you're done, press "Cash Out" and take your printed ticket to another machine or the cashier.

6. Playing Table Games

Table games (blackjack, craps, roulette) can feel intimidating but the dealers are trained to help beginners. Approach a table with a minimum bet you're comfortable with (typically $5–$25). Place your cash on the table (never hand it to the dealer) and they'll give you chips. Don't be afraid to ask the dealer to explain the game — they do it all day.

Beginner tip: Blackjack is the best table game for beginners — the rules are simple, the pace is manageable, and the house edge is among the lowest in the casino. Ask the dealer for advice on when to hit or stand — they won't steer you wrong.

7. Casino Etiquette

Don't touch other people's chips or machines. Don't use your phone at the table. Tip your dealer when you win (it's customary, not required). Be respectful of other players' space. Don't celebrate too loudly at someone else's loss. Dress casually — jeans and a shirt are fine everywhere in Oklahoma.

Rules & Age Requirements

Before You Go

Age Requirements

Most Oklahoma casinos are 18+ for the gaming floor. Some properties or specific areas (certain bars, nightclubs) require 21+. Non-gaming areas like restaurants and The District at Choctaw are generally open to all ages. Always verify the age requirement with the specific casino before visiting — rules can vary by property and by area within the property.

What to Bring

  • Valid photo ID (driver's license, passport, or military ID)
  • Cash — withdraw from your own bank to avoid casino ATM fees
  • A set budget you're comfortable losing entirely
  • Comfortable shoes — casino floors are big

What NOT to Do

  • Don't bring more money than your budget — leave extra cards in the car
  • Don't chase losses — if you lose your budget, stop and enjoy the resort
  • Don't drink too much — alcohol impairs judgment and leads to bigger losses
  • Don't forget to sign up for rewards — it's free money you're leaving behind
