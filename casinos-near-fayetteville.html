NWA Visitor Guide

Fayetteville to Oklahoma — What to Know

Northwest Arkansas is one of the fastest-growing regions in the country — but it doesn't have casinos. The good news: Oklahoma is right next door, and you have three solid options within a reasonable drive from the Fayetteville-Springdale-Rogers metro.

Cherokee Roland — The Closest Option

Cherokee Casino Roland is about an hour south via I-49 and I-40. It's a Cherokee Nation property near the Arkansas border with a gaming floor, poker room, and table games. It's the most convenient option for a quick evening trip from NWA — no overnight stay needed.

Hard Rock Tulsa — The Upgrade

If you want a bigger experience, Hard Rock Hotel & Casino Tulsa is about 1 hour 45 minutes west via US-412. This is a full resort: 2,600+ slots, poker room, 454-room hotel, championship golf course, and Hard Rock Live — one of the best concert venues in the region. It's worth the extra drive if you're planning an overnight trip.

💡 Pro Tip: If you're driving from NWA to Hard Rock Tulsa, take US-412 west — it's the most direct route and scenic through the Ozark foothills. The Muskogee Turnpike from I-40 is an alternative but takes longer from Fayetteville.

Downstream — The Weekend Getaway

Downstream Casino Resort sits at the tri-state border about 2 hours 15 minutes north via I-49. It's a solid weekend destination with a hotel, table games, and regular concert events. The drive goes through Joplin, MO — a good stop for lunch on the way.
